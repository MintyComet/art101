Pseudocode an Everyday Task:

How to Make a Japanese Cake Roll:

  1. Preheat an oven to 350°F/ 176°C and prepare a 9x13 in. baking tray lined with parchment paper.
  2. In a bowl, whisk egg yolks, sugar, salt, vanilla, oil, and milk together.
  3. Using a sieve, sift cake flour and baking powder into the egg yolk mixture then whisk together.
  4. In a large mixing bowl, wipe the bowl with some lemon juice or vinegar using a paper towel. Add the egg whites and beat with an electric mixer on high until frothy.
    1. Add cream of tartar and beat until whites are opaque and the bubbles have tightened up.
    2. Add sugar and beat until the egg whites hold stiff peaks when you lift the mixer out of the bowl.
  5. Use a rubber spatula to add ⅓ of the egg white meringue into the egg yolk mixture and gently fold until it’s mostly mixed. Repeat for the remaining thirds.
  6. Pour the batter into the baking tray and tap the tray a few times on a counter to release any air bubbles.
  7. Bake for 15-17 min or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.
  8. After taking the cake out of the oven, quickly flip it over onto a slightly damp kitchen towel or another piece of parchment paper that is larger than the cake.
    1. Peel off the parchment paper and roll the cake slowly starting from the short side of the cake. Allow cake to cool completely while it’s rolled up.
  9. To make the whipped cream, use an electric mixer with the whisk attachment to mix together heavy cream, sugar, and vanilla extract until medium to stiff peaks form.
  10. After the cake has cooled, unroll and trim the edges.
    1. Spread whipped cream and add fruits or anything else on top if desired.
    2. Gently roll cake back up without the towel/paper.
    3. Wrap the cake with plastic wrap and chill it in the fridge for 1 hour. It can be served after this.

Pseudocode a Computer Task:

Searching for a book on a library website:

  1. Prompt user to input whether they are searching by title or author.
    1. Display a drop down select box.
    2. Display text telling the user to select from the two options.
  2. Prompt user to type in their search text.
    1. Display a text box for input.
    2. Display text telling the user to type their search.
  3. Accept user input.
    1. Read the option selected from the drop down menu.
    2. Read the text inputted by the user in the text box.
  4. Compare the input text to the library lists of the type selected.
    1. Evaluate whether the user chose author or title.
    2. Check if any text in the library’s data matches the text inputted by the user.
    3. Check if any information partially matches the user input.
  5. Sort resulting information for display.
    1. Organize information results from closest matches to partial matches.
    2. Sort data into a vertically listed format.
  6. Display information or that it is not found.
    1. If there was one or more exact matches for the search, display the organized list.
    2. If there was no exact match, display a message saying so and also the partially matching results.
